Editor's Note

One of the best classes I ever took was "Birth and Death of Stars". I was in my final year and I scored a C- (the minimal passing grade). To be frank, I think I actually failed. But, the teacher saw "History" behind my name and gave me pity points for trying hard. I loved that class — I just suck at math.

The point is, every now and then a story reminds us of the tech community's greatest strength — the embrace of curiosity. Yesterday, no story made more waves than NASA's discovery of 7 Earth-size planets hosted by a nearby star.

It was a reminder that people everywhere are accomplishing great things, driven in large part by their curiosity. So if you're interested in space, but terrible at math, take a class. You'll probably score badly, but that's not really the point, is it?

Oh yeah, and some dude sold a [bleeping] ship on Taobao this week.
